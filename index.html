Unfit

A psychological domestic thriller about grief, control, and the fragile line between care and coercion.

Nathan Hale leaves the house for a routine errand. When he returns, his pregnant wife is dead. The evidence calls it suicide. The timeline makes sense—except nothing feels right. As Nathan’s grief deepens, Maria’s family steps in with answers he can’t prove wrong. The more exhausted he becomes, the more unreliable he appears. And the harder it is to tell whether his doubts are instinct—or desperation.

Interwoven with the present are glimpses of Maria in the months before her death: a woman preparing for motherhood while navigating expectations she never agreed to, and a family whose love carries conditions she doesn’t know how to escape.

About Kelsea

Kelsea Conrad is a thriller writer and English teacher who believes a great story is a perfectly timed reveal, a character who feels real, and a moment that makes you say, “Oh no,” right before you keep reading anyway.

Under the name Finally Creative, she’s on a mission to finish the stories that have shaped her life—and help others fall in love with writing along the way. She notices what people don’t say, what they avoid, what they hide in plain sight, and uses those quiet details to build psychological pressure and twists that feel inevitable once you see them.

When she isn’t writing, she’s usually reading like it’s research, collecting story sparks in everyday life, or talking craft with students who don’t realize they’re learning the same skills every great thriller depends on: precision, suspense, and the power of the right detail at the right time.
